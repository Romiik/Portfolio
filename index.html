    Jun 2021

    On Using Natural Language Processing to Measure Gender-Specific Character Exposure in Pre-Modern English Literature

    PLoS ONE, 2021

    In this article, we address three hypotheses specifically concerning gender-specific character exposure in pre-modern literature (books published approximately between 1800-1950), acquired from the publicly available Project Gutenberg corpus. Our findings show the significant discrepancy between female character exposure and male character exposure in literature. They also show that the discrepancy declines when controlling for the gender of the author. The discrepancy has not diminished over time. Our experimental methodology and materials also serve as a case study in how modern processes in NLP can be used to rigorously answer important questions in digital humanities and computational social science.
